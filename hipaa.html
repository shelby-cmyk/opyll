Notice of privacy practices.

Last updated · 2026-05-20

This is a placeholder. Before launch, Opyll's clinical entity will publish a complete HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices for protected health information (PHI), consistent with 45 CFR § 164.520.

What this notice will cover

  • How Opyll's affiliated clinical practice and pharmacy partner may use and disclose your PHI for treatment, payment, and healthcare operations
  • Your rights with respect to your PHI — access, amendment, accounting of disclosures, restrictions, confidential communications
  • Required disclosures (to you, to HHS for compliance review)
  • Permitted disclosures (public health, law enforcement, etc.) with the conditions that apply
  • How we protect your PHI and what we do if there is a breach
  • Our responsibility to provide notice of breach affecting unsecured PHI
  • How to file a complaint with Opyll or with HHS Office for Civil Rights

Marketing and tracking

Opyll separates its consumer-facing marketing operations from its clinical operations. Information collected on the marketing site (including email captures, attribution parameters, and analytics) is not PHI and is governed by our Privacy Policy. Once you become a patient, your information is PHI and governed by this Notice.
